Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India 🇮🇳

About

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. He was widely known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. His humble personality and dedication towards students made him one of the most loved presidents in India.

Life Timeline

  • 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • 1960: Graduated in Aerospace Engineering
  • 1980: Led India's SLV-III project
  • 1998: Key role in Pokhran-II nuclear tests
  • 2002–2007: President of India
  • 2015: Passed away while delivering a lecture

Major Achievements

  • Developed India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III)
  • Chief architect of India's missile programs
  • Awarded Bharat Ratna
  • Inspired millions of youth

Why "Missile Man of India"?

Dr. Kalam earned the title "Missile Man of India" for his significant contributions to the development of ballistic missile technology, including projects like Agni and Prithvi missiles.
